How to read this. The big number is the probability engine: a probit on the 10-year minus 3-month Treasury spread alone (the Estrella-Mishkin / NY Fed standard). The fragility signals do not change that probability — they grade the character of the risk (is a 2008-style credit-cycle collapse brewing?). An inverted curve always warns through the probability; the model never declares a "false alarm." Thresholds shown are documented judgment calls, not fitted parameters. The two lines on the chart are the point: the rosy fitted line has seen all history; the prominent real-time line shows what the model would actually have said month by month — its AUC is the honest headline.
