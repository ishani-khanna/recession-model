Recession conditions matrix — which ingredients were in place at each inversion

Each row is a historical yield-curve inversion (at its deepest month), plus a live Today row. Compare 2007 and 2023: both inverted with falling real house prices, but household leverage was 133% of income in 2007 versus 95% in 2023. That leverage gap is the cleanest single piece of evidence for why 2022–24 inverted yet produced no recession.

How to read this. The big number is the probability engine — a probit on the 10-year minus 3-month Treasury spread alone (the Estrella-Mishkin / NY Fed standard). It gives a probability, not a certainty, and a deep inversion can be a false alarm. The leading explanation for the 2022–24 episode is term-premium compression: when the term premium is unusually low (even negative), a given inversion overstates recession odds relative to the history the model was trained on — so the raw curve signal should be read conditioned on the term premium (see the term-premium trust check). The fragility signals and the conditions matrix below are supporting real-economy context — the 2007-vs-2023 leverage contrast — not the headline. Thresholds shown are documented judgment calls, not fitted parameters. The two lines on the chart are the point: the rosy fitted line has seen all history; the prominent real-time line shows what the model would actually have said month by month — its AUC is the honest headline.
