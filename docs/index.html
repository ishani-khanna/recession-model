The character banner uses only the three variables in the verdict rule: debt-to-income, real house prices, and the credit spread. The other three (debt growth, SLOOS, migration) shape the "most resembles [year]" match below, but do not change the banner — by design.

Recession conditions matrix — which ingredients were in place at each inversion

Each row is a historical yield-curve inversion (at its deepest month), plus a live Today row. Compare 2007 and 2023: both inverted with falling real house prices, but household leverage was 133% of income in 2007 versus 95% in 2023. That leverage gap is the cleanest single piece of evidence for why 2022–24 inverted yet produced no recession.

How to read this. The big number is the probability engine: a probit on the 10-year minus 3-month Treasury spread alone (the Estrella-Mishkin / NY Fed standard). The fragility signals do not change that probability — they grade the character of the risk (is a 2008-style credit-cycle collapse brewing?). An inverted curve always warns through the probability; the model never declares a "false alarm." Thresholds shown are documented judgment calls, not fitted parameters. The two lines on the chart are the point: the rosy fitted line has seen all history; the prominent real-time line shows what the model would actually have said month by month — its AUC is the honest headline.
